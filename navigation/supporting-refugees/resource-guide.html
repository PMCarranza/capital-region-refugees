The Capital Region Refugee Roundtable is an active group of more than 300 volunteers who do a variety of activities to serve and support refugees and immigrants. Regular meetings are attended by mentors, volunteers, and representatives from local organizations and New York State agencies, educational and health care institutions, law enforcement and legal services, and the City School District of Albany. One of the best ways to find out about resources in the Capital Region is to explore the Resource Guide found HERE.

To better understand the most successful ways to assist refugees, the following Albany, NY organizations can provide training and resources:

U.S. Committee for Refugees and Immigrants (Albany Office)

For further guidelines on building self-sufficiency and/or mentoring refugee families, see the USCRI Refugee Mentor Manual here.

Additional organizations listed below.

Albany Law School Chapter of the , in partnership with Women Against War (WAW), utilizes law students and attorneys to provide legal representation to Iraqi refugees who need assistance with the legal system. For more information, e-mail: lpinc@albanylaw.edu.

Capital Region BOCES provides a variety of ESL courses that adult refugees can register for. They also offer High School Equivalency classes. For more information, call 862-4900.

Center for the Elimination of Minority Health Disparities (CEMHD) is a collaborative effort focusing on minority health disparities in the smaller cities and towns of New York. CEMHD works toward eliminating health disparities by developing capacity in faculty at the University of Albany and partnering with community groups to identify community health concerns and sources of disparities, plan strategies to alleviate them, and then test their effectiveness. For more information, contact Lawrence M. Schell (Center Director) at 442-4904 or  
e-mail: CEMHD@Albany.edu.

Child Care Facilitated Enrollment Program at the Workforce Development Institute provides child care for children under 13 years of age to income eligible working families in the Capital Region. For more information, call 272-3530 (Albany, Rensselaer, and Schenectady counties).

Crime Victim and Sexual Violence Center provides direct and comprehensive services to all victims of sexual assault and personal violence to change, through community and prevention education and bystander engagement, the societal conditions that allow oppression, especially interpersonal violence, to exist. Treatment is offered to groups, individuals, and families. The Center operates a 24-hour crisis hotline for supportive counseling and facilitates access to the hospital and/or police station for victims of sexual violence. For more information, call 447-7100 or e-mail: cvsv@albanycounty.com.

Hudson Valley Community College (HVCC) is based in Troy and serves approximately 13,250 students through 75 associate degree and certificate programs. Currently the second-largest institution of higher learning in the Capital Region, Hudson Valley is ranked among the top 10 percent of community colleges in the nation for student success and completion. The HVCC Educational Opportunity Center (EOC) offers ESL courses, High School Equivalency courses, and College Prep courses with locations in Albany and Troy. The college ranks 36th nationally in the number of associate degrees awarded annually, and has more than 70,000 alumni. For more information, call the HVCC EOC at 273-1900 or e-mail: EOCinfo@hvcc.edu. 

Literacy New York of the Greater Capital Region (LYGCR) provides Adult ESOL classes. Trained volunteers provide English language instruction to small groups. Literacy NY has an Office for New Americans (ONA) program that provides ESOL classes and citizenship assistance to clients. For more information, call 583-1232 or e-mail: info@literacynycap.org. 
​
Literacy Volunteers of Rensselaer County (LVORC) provided free, confidential tutoring to adults in reading, writing, and English for speakers of other languages (ESOL). LVORC recruits and trains volunteers to be tutors. Based in Troy, LVORC serves more than 200 learners annually, with programs including one-to-one tutoring and small group instruction in reading and writing, math, ESOL, financial literacy, computer literacy, workplace literacy, citizenship preparation and reading mentors for kids in elementary school. For more information, call 274-8526.

MAMI Interpreters offers interpreting and translation services in over 50 languages seven days a week, 24-hours a day (including holidays). All MAMI interpreters comply with HIPAA regulations and are covered with liability insurance. They receive training which qualifies them to interpret in medical, social services, as well as some legal settings and courts. MAMI also provides free trainings to providers on how to work with an interpreter and negotiate with a patient/client so that he/she will be more likely to comply with a request from the provider. In addition, MAMI conducts customized cultural competency trainings. For more information, call 426-1626 or e-mail: info@mamiinterpreters.org.

New York State Migrant Program was established in 1975 to help school districts in New York State meet the unique needs of migrant children. Migrant program staff provide academic support including one-on-one tutoring, small group instruction, home based academic assistance, and ESL services. In addition, staff provide outreach programs which include: PASS Program, Summer Leadership Conference, Career Retreats, English Language Immersion Project, and the College Assisted Migrant Program. New York State Migrant staff also provide advocacy related services to migrant students.

NYS Department of Labor, Division of Immigrant Policies and Affairs (DIPA) addresses the needs, issues, and challenges of immigrants by providing outreach in immigrant communities, reviewing policies, procedures and laws that affect immigrants, and monitoring compliance with labor laws by farms and farmers in the state. DIPA works inside and outside the Department of Labor to serve the needs of immigrants. Their protections, services, and programs are available to everyone, including people with limited English proficiency. DIPA teaches immigrant communities about their workplace rights, and the agency's services and programs. For more information, call 1-877-466-9757 or e-mail: dipa@labor.ny.gov.

The New York State Office for New Americans (ONA) was launched in 2013 and assists newcomers who are eager to contribute to our economy and become part of the family of NY State. The foundation of the office is a network of 27 neighborhood-based ‘Opportunity Centers’ located throughout the State. These Opportunity Centers, hosted within existing culturally-competent, language-accessible, community-based organizations, provide new Americans with opportunities including ESOL classes, naturalization assistance, and informational seminars on entrepreneurship and other relevant issues. Additionally, each center is supported by a team of rotating attorneys, who are knowledgeable about immigration law. Furthermore, the New York State New Americans Hotline (1-800-566-7636) is a multi-lingual information center providing live assistance in more than 200 languages on general questions related to immigration and naturalization. To find your nearest Opportunity Center, visit the ONA website: http://www.newamericans.ny.gov/opportunity/opportunity_centers.html

Trinity Alliance is a local agency that provides community services to promote and support healthy families, adults, and children.  Trinity is dedicated to improving the neighborhood setting for family life, contributing to health and well-being, and promoting education and employment as a means of self-development. Trinity Alliance is also home to the Literacy Zone, a program working with Capital Region BOCES and Literacy NY, ONA to provide case management and ESL courses to refugees. For more information, call 449-5155.

WMHT has partnered with the Refugee Roundtable through Community Conversations, and in their attendance of roundtable meetings in order to understand how their place in the community intersects with the work of the Roundtable. WMHT places the community at the center of their work and believe that the importance of their services demands that they be carried out with excellence.  More than just a television station, WMHT strives to support and represent the community in all of its work. To contact WMHT, call 880-3400.
