Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program
The Supplemental Nutrition Assistance Program (SNAP), which is administered by the United States Department of Agriculture (USDA), is geared toward enabling low-income households to purchase the food they need to maintain proper nutritional levels and good health. The Thrifty Food Plan is the USDA's estimate of a family's total monthly food expense broken down by household size which is adjusted annually in accordance with the cost of living index.

Medicaid 
Medicaid is a medical assistance program jointly financed by the federal, state and local government for low-income individuals. Eligibility is based on income and resource guidelines as well as certain other criteria. Medicaid provides medical coverage for people who are unable to pay for their medical care and who have income below the eligibility guidelines set by the State (New York State Medicaid FAQ).

Beyond the First Eight Months: Right now, many refugees get short-term health insurance called Refugee Medical Assistance (RMA), available for up to eight months. Beyond this period, some refugees may be eligible for Medicaid or the Childrenâ€™s Health Insurance Program (CHIP). However, under the new Affordable Care Act (ACA) health care law, the health insurance landscape for refugees and other Americans is changing. Many refugees, who could only get eight months of insurance through RMA, will now be able to get ongoing health insurance through the Marketplace.
How to Get Health Insurance at the Marketplace: The Affordable Care Act created the Marketplace in order to help people shop for lower cost health insurance. The Marketplace helps individuals determine if they are eligible for programs like Medicaid and the Children's Health Insurance Program (CHIP). No matter where you live, you will only need to fill out a single application on the Marketplace. You can access the Marketplace online here, or by phone at 1-800-318-2596.

The Office of Refugee Resettlement wants to make sure that refugees and service providers know how to navigate the health insurance landscape, and has developed tools to help with outreach and enrollment activities. The ORR produced a six-minute video to introduce the Health Insurance Marketplace to refugees, available in six languages available here. 

Temporary Assistance
Temporary Assistance (TA) a broad term used in New York State to describe a range of public assistance (welfare) programs. TA may provide benefits and support for those who do not have sufficient income to pay for basic needs. DSS staff works with individuals and families to achieve self-sufficiency and independence. This may include assistance with identification and removal of barriers to employment and provision of resources to support employment readiness. (For additional information, view the Office Of Temporary and Disability Assistance Temporary Assistance Overview page).
